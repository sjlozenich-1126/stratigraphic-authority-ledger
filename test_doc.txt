Case Details 
Case No. 22‑1‑04347‑2SEA, The State of Washington vs. Shane Lozenich, arises from an alleged incident involving a reported threat made during a period of heightened institutional scrutiny and ongoing legal instability. The case was filed in King County Superior Court and assigned to Judge Michael Scott, with the charge centered on a single statement that was interpreted by authorities as a criminal threat despite the absence of corroborating evidence, contextual analysis, or a documented victim impact. The matter proceeded through a series of procedural irregularities, including delayed discovery, inconsistent representation, and the introduction of psychiatric evaluations that attempted to reframe the defendant’s statements as symptoms rather than communicative acts. Although the case was initiated in 2022, it remained unresolved for an extended period, with no trial, plea, or dismissal, placing the defendant in prolonged legal limbo. The case functions as a representative example of how low‑threshold allegations can escalate into full criminal proceedings when institutional actors rely on administrative momentum rather than evidentiary grounding. 
Executive Summary 
Case No. 21‑1‑04347‑2 SEA represents a critical intersection of digital surveillance, anonymity privilege, and procedural breakdown within King County Superior Court. The case originated from a July 23, 2021 arrest executed by unidentified agents in unmarked vehicles, followed by allegations of felony harassment and cyberstalking. A major systemic failure occurred on September 13, 2021, when Judge Melinda Young issued a 45‑day restoration order without a competency evaluation and without defense counsel present.
Initial adjudication involved forced medication, warrantless seizure of digital devices, and the production of personal documents the defendant states he never possessed. By February 2026, however, forensic evaluator Dr. Jamie Leavey determined that the defendant’s competency had been restored following outpatient treatment, marking a dramatic reversal of the state’s earlier clinical narrative.
This case illustrates how clinical framing was used to substitute for legal process, allowing the state to delay scrutiny of constitutional violations while reframing the defendant’s archival research as “cyberstalking” or “grandiosity.”
